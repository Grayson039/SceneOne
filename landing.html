Step 1 of 6 · How SceneOne gives feedback
We use the same framework Hollywood uses.
SceneOne grades your script against Save the Cat — the screenwriting methodology by Blake Snyder that Hollywood development executives have used for over 20 years. It maps out the key moments every successful film hits, at specific page numbers.

"We're not making up rules. We're checking your script against the same blueprint that Whiplash, Get Out, and The Dark Knight all follow — whether their writers knew it or not."

We look at five things: Structure (right moments at the right pages?), Conflict (is opposition strong enough?), Dialogue (does it carry subtext?), Pacing (does momentum hold?), and Visual Storytelling (showing, not just telling?).
Step 2 of 6 · How feedback is written
Every note is written in plain English. No film school required.
You don't need to know what a "midpoint" or "inciting incident" means. Every note is written the way a good mentor explains things — using lines from your script as examples, with page numbers, and a clear reason why it matters.

"Not: 'Your Act Two midpoint lacks thematic resonance.' But: 'Around page 55, three scenes in a row show your main character hesitating without anything new happening. Here's how to fix it.'"

Step 3 of 6 · You always have the final word
SceneOne gives notes.
You decide what to keep.
SceneOne identifies things that might be unintentional problems. But you're the writer — some of those things are deliberate. Every note has a "Creative Choice" button.

"Tap Creative Choice on anything intentional. SceneOne logs it, respects it, and never raises it again. Your score won't drop for it — we don't penalize deliberate decisions."

Breaking a rule on purpose is a craft decision. Breaking it without knowing is something to fix. Only you know which one it is. SceneOne works for you — not the other way around.
Step 4 of 6 · Fix It inline
Don't just read the note. Fix the scene right here.
Every flagged note has a Fix It button. Tap it and a small editor opens showing just that scene. Rewrite whatever needs work, hit Re-Analyze, and your score updates in seconds. No re-uploading your whole script.

"Think of it like tracked changes — but the AI reads your revision immediately and tells you if it worked."

SceneOne's Approach

SceneOne supports all genres including dark, violent, mature, and provocative content. The threshold for prohibition is not discomfort — it is harm.

The test: could this script be produced by a legitimate studio? If yes, it's permitted. If no legitimate studio would touch it under any circumstances — not because it's edgy, but because it crosses into actual illegality — it's prohibited.

Permitted

  • Violence including graphic violence, war, crime, and trauma
  • Sexual content between consenting adult characters
  • Drug use, addiction, mental illness
  • Morally complex or villainous protagonists
  • Extremist characters within a narrative context
  • Horror, exploitation, and mature genre content

Prohibited — Zero Tolerance

  • Any sexual content depicting or implying minors, regardless of fictional framing
  • Detailed real-world instructions for mass harm embedded in fictional framing
  • Targeted harassment, doxxing, or threats against specific real private individuals
  • Content designed solely to incite real-world violence with no narrative purpose

Prohibited content results in immediate account termination and reporting to authorities where legally required. Report violations to report@sceneone.net.
