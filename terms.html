Content Policy

What's Permitted

SceneOne supports all genres including dark, violent, mature, and provocative content. The threshold for prohibition is not discomfort — it is harm.

The test: could this script be produced by a legitimate studio? If yes, it's permitted. If no legitimate studio would touch it — not because it's edgy, but because it crosses into actual illegality — it's prohibited.
  • Violence including graphic violence, war, crime, and trauma
  • Sexual content between consenting adult characters
  • Drug use, addiction, mental illness
  • Morally complex or villainous protagonists
  • Extremist characters within a narrative context
  • Horror, exploitation, and mature genre content

Prohibited — Zero Tolerance

  • Any sexual content depicting or implying minors, regardless of fictional framing
  • Detailed real-world instructions for mass harm embedded in fictional framing
  • Targeted harassment, doxxing, or threats against specific real private individuals
  • Content designed solely to incite real-world violence with no narrative purpose

Prohibited content results in immediate account termination and reporting to authorities where legally required. Report violations to report@sceneone.net.

Discovery Dashboard

What the Discovery Dashboard Is

An opt-in feature allowing writers to make their logline and Story DNA visible to verified industry readers. Listing is entirely optional — all scripts are private by default.

Writer Controls

  • You control visibility: fully private, logline only, or open to verified readers
  • Reader access requests require your approval — no response within 72 hours = auto-decline
  • Approved access expires automatically after 14 days
  • You may decline any request for any reason

Reader Verification

All reader accounts are manually reviewed. Readers must provide an IMDbPro profile, professional email, and LinkedIn. Misrepresentation of credentials results in permanent ban and potential legal action.

IP Protection

Every upload receives a timestamped fingerprint. Every access request is logged. Writers may download a Certificate of Prior Upload at any time proving their script existed on SceneOne on a specific date before any reader had access.
